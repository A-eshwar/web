Turtles and Tortoises as Pets

Red-Eared Slider

One of the most popular pet turtles, red-eared sliders are aquatic and need a tank with both water and basking areas. They are active swimmers and enjoy a mix of meat, greens, and pellets. With proper care, they can live over 20 years and require strong filtration to keep their water clean.

Russian Tortoise

Russian tortoises are small, hardy, and suitable for beginners. They are land-dwelling and herbivorous, enjoying leafy greens. These tortoises are social, enjoy digging and exploring, and thrive in enclosures with dry, sandy soil and lots of sunlight.

Box Turtle

Box turtles are terrestrial and live long lives, often over 40 years. They require a secure outdoor enclosure or large indoor terrarium with UV light and a diet of insects, veggies, and fruit. Their shells close tightly for protection, and they prefer high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Leopard Tortoise

Leopard tortoises are large, slow-moving reptiles with beautiful shell patterns. They are peaceful, herbivorous, and do best in warm, dry climates. These tortoises do not burrow, making them suitable for grassy enclosures, but they need space and protection from predators.

Yellow-Bellied Slider

This turtle species is often confused with the red-eared slider but has a yellow patch on its shell. Active and hardy, they love basking and swimming. Good filtration is essential in their tank, and they enjoy a mix of protein and aquatic plants. They grow large and need a spacious habitat.

Greek Tortoise

Greek tortoises are calm, charming, and make great companions. With a modest size and gentle nature, they do well in indoor or outdoor enclosures with plenty of sunlight and veggies. Their domed shell provides good protection, and they are ideal for owners looking for a low-stress reptile pet.

African Sideneck Turtle

African Sidenecks are freshwater turtles known for their long necks and curious personalities. They need a warm aquatic environment with a basking spot and UV lighting. They are social but may nip if not handled carefully. These turtles often prefer shallow water with hiding spots and driftwood.

Learn more on Wikipedia

Sulcata Tortoise

Sulcata tortoises are one of the largest tortoise species and can weigh over 100 pounds. They are herbivores with a big appetite and need a lot of space. Ideal for owners with large yards and warm climates, they are strong diggers and require secure fencing to prevent escapes. They can live for over 70 years!
