Squirrels as Pets

Eastern Gray Squirrel

Common in North America, the Eastern Gray Squirrel is intelligent and playful. If hand-raised from infancy, they can become tame and affectionate. They require a lot of space, mental stimulation, and secure housing as they are natural climbers and chewers. Check local wildlife laws before considering one as a pet.

Red Squirrel

Red squirrels are highly energetic, fast-moving, and vocal. They are smaller than gray squirrels and known for their feisty nature. Red squirrels are best for experienced caregivers who can handle their high energy levels and occasional territorial behavior. They require climbing branches and foraging toys to stay mentally active.

Prevost's Squirrel

With its striking tricolor fur, the Prevost's squirrel is one of the most beautiful species. Native to Southeast Asia, they are exotic and require special care. These squirrels are very agile and need large vertical cages. Though not typically cuddly, their beauty and activity make them a fascinating pet to observe.

Indian Palm Squirrel

Native to India and Sri Lanka, this striped squirrel is small, fast, and intelligent. They adapt well to human environments and can be friendly if socialized early. They thrive in warm climates and prefer large enclosures or supervised outdoor time. A good choice for patient owners.

Japanese Dwarf Flying Squirrel

This adorable gliding squirrel is nocturnal and shy, making it ideal for quiet homes. It requires a tall, secure cage to glide safely and prefers a calm, routine-based environment. Bonding takes time, but their wide eyes and gentle presence make them a favorite among exotic pet lovers.

Siberian Flying Squirrel

Known for their soft fur and wide gliding range, Siberian flying squirrels are rare but gentle. They're nocturnal, requiring quiet during the day and interaction at night. With the right environment and diet, they form close bonds with their owners. Suitable for exotic pet enthusiasts.

Learn more on Wikipedia

Southern Flying Squirrel

This U.S. native glider is tiny, affectionate, and social. Southern flying squirrels are very loyal if hand-raised and often ride in pouches or pockets. They are great for indoor bonding but need a safe space to glide and explore. Best kept in pairs or groups to prevent loneliness.
